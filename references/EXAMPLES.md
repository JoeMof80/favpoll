# favpoll Placeholder Examples

Six worked examples from the demo scenes. These are the quality bar.
Every character brief, framing, and reveal here passed review.
Use them as anchors when generating new copy.

---

## 1. Belinda Hartley — Memorial

**Character brief**
- Name: Belinda Hartley
- Age / life stage: 79, retired primary school headmistress
- Occasion: Memorial
- Key detail: Taught for 41 years at the same school in Shrewsbury. Knew every
  pupil's name by the end of the first week. Often wore purple — naturally,
  not as a statement.
- Charity: Marie Curie — their nurses visited Belinda at home in her final
  weeks. Her daughter Rachel created the favpoll.
- Topic: Colour (finite)
- Their answer: Purple
- Reveal note: Written in Belinda's voice — Rachel found a note written for
  her own eulogy and used it as the framing. First person feels right here.

**Framing:** "Belinda had a colour she always wore — do you?"
**Reveal:** "Mine was purple. I wore it to every occasion that mattered."

**Why this works:** First person suits the memorial register. The detail
("every occasion that mattered") is specific without being overwrought.
Marie Curie is there because they were present at her death — not a guess.

## 2. Poppy Chen — Birthday

**Character brief**
- Name: Poppy Chen
- Age / life stage: Turning 30, UX designer in Bristol, originally from Singapore
- Occasion: Birthday (30th)
- Key detail: Extremely opinionated about food, completely unpretentious about
  it. Mint choc chip is her position and she has held it for years.
- Charity: Great Ormond Street Hospital — Poppy's younger brother was treated
  there as a child. The family have supported them ever since.
- Topic: Ice cream (infinite)
- Their answer: Mint choc chip
- Reveal note: Her voice — direct, slightly mock-outraged. The reveal should
  sound like her talking.

**Framing:** "Tell us your favourite ice cream to find out Poppy's."
**Reveal:** "Mint choc chip, of course. What do you mean you didn't know?"

**Why this works:** Withheld reveal pattern — the framing is pure tease.
The reveal voice is specific to Poppy: confident, funny, not sentimental.
GOSH is a family loyalty, not a gesture.

## 3. Ros Turner — Retirement

**Character brief**
- Name: Ros Turner (married name — husband is Japanese-British, met at a
  librarians' conference in 2001)
- Age / life stage: 63, retiring secondary school librarian after 35 years
  at the same comprehensive in Exeter
- Occasion: Retirement
- Key detail: Quiet, precise, fond of long walks. Not sentimental about
  leaving — she's ready. Her husband has made her packed lunches every day
  since he retired two years ago.
- Charity: The Woodland Trust — she's been a member for twenty years. Once
  organised a school trip to plant trees that are now taller than the students
  who planted them.
- Topic: Season (finite)
- Their answer: Autumn
- Reveal note: Third person, settled and quiet. Not elegiac — she is not
  dying, she is finishing work. The reveal should feel contented, not final.

**Framing:** "Which time of year do you love? Is it the same as Ros's?"
**Reveal:** "Autumn, always. She never needed a reason."

**Why this works:** "Is it the same as Ros's?" is the indirect tease pattern
— competitive without being pushy. The reveal is quiet. "She never needed a
reason" is the opposite of over-constructed — it trusts the reader.
The Woodland Trust connection is real and specific (twenty years, the trees).

## 4. Alex & Jordan — Engagement

**Character brief**
- Name: Alex & Jordan
- Age / life stage: Late twenties, newly engaged
- Occasion: Engagement
- Key detail: Met in 2019 volunteering at a giant panda conservation centre in
  Chengdu. Jordan proposed eighteen months later. They have told the Chengdu
  story many times.
- Charity: WWF — the origin story of their relationship. The charity is not
  a gesture; it is where they met.
- Topic: Animal (infinite)
- Their answer: Giant panda
- Reveal note: Third person, warm, earns the right to be slightly knowing.
  The reveal should make guests who know them smile.

**Framing:** "What's your favourite animal? Alex & Jordan have one..."
**Reveal:** "The giant panda. They met at a conservation centre in Chengdu.
Did you really need to ask?"

**Why this works:** The trailing ellipsis in the framing creates suspense
without being coy. The reveal earns its "Did you really need to ask?" because
the backstory justifies it — guests who know them will have guessed. WWF is
the only possible charity given the story.

## 5. Dave Kowalski — Leaving do

**Character brief**
- Name: Dave Kowalski
- Age / life stage: 34, leaving a marketing agency in Manchester after eight
  years to go freelance. Polish-British.
- Occasion: Leaving do
- Key detail: Organised the office Red Nose Day collection every year without
  being asked. Universally liked. Kept a Bourbon stash in his desk drawer that
  everyone knew about and nobody mentioned.
- Charity: Comic Relief — Dave's thing. He never made a big deal of it.
  The team did.
- Topic: Biscuit (infinite)
- Their answer: Bourbon
- Reveal note: Third person, warm, slightly funny. The detail of the desk
  drawer is specific and true. The reveal should feel like an affectionate
  in-joke.

**Framing:** "Dave has had strong opinions about biscuits for eight years —
what's your opinion?"
**Reveal:** "Bourbon. He kept a secret stash in his desk drawer and everyone knew."

**Why this works:** "What's your opinion?" mirrors and completes "strong
opinions" in the framing — a small piece of craft. The reveal is a detail,
not a sentiment. Comic Relief is earned — he did the collection every year.

## 6. James Okafor — Graduation

**Character brief**
- Name: James Okafor
- Age / life stage: 22, just finished mechanical engineering at Leeds.
  From Peckham, first in his family to go to university.
- Occasion: Graduation
- Key detail: Received a Prince's Trust development award at sixteen that
  funded a summer engineering programme — the thing that made him apply to
  university. Going into renewable energy.
- Charity: The Prince's Trust — direct. The Trust helped make this graduation
  possible. The favpoll is created by his mum.
- Topic: Film (infinite)
- Their answer: Jurassic Park
- Reveal note: Third person, specific to his story. The reveal should connect
  the film to why he became an engineer — not sentimental, just true.
  His housemates are a grounding detail.

**Framing:** "James has a favourite film he could watch again and again — do you?"
**Reveal:** "Jurassic Park. He said it was the reason he applied to study
engineering. His housemates were not surprised."

**Why this works:** The framing is the simplest possible withheld invitation.
The reveal earns its weight by connecting the film to the occasion — graduation
— without spelling it out. "His housemates were not surprised" is specific and
human. The Prince's Trust connection is direct: they funded the programme that
led to this day.

## Patterns to carry forward

| Pattern | Example |
|---|---|
| Framing withholds, reveal discloses | Belinda (colour), James (film) |
| Framing teases with trailing ellipsis | Alex & Jordan (animal) |
| Framing withholds via "tell us X to find out Y's" | Poppy (ice cream) |
| Framing uses indirect comparison | Ros ("Is it the same as Ros's?") |
| Reveal in protagonist's own voice | Belinda ("Mine was purple") |
| Reveal in third person with one grounding detail | Dave ("He kept a secret stash") |
| Reveal earns a rhetorical question | Alex & Jordan ("Did you really need to ask?") |
| Reveal connects answer to occasion | James (film → engineering → graduation) |
| Charity earned by a named personal connection | All six |
| Charity earned by the origin story of the relationship | Alex & Jordan (WWF / Chengdu) |
