# favpoll — For Organisers

## A collection with meaning

When someone we love dies, we want to do something that feels worthy of them. Not just pass a tin. Not just post a link.

favpoll turns a memorial collection into something people actually remember. Guests pledge donations against their favourite things — their favourite colour, song, season — and the answers build a living picture of what the people in that room love most. The money goes to a charity chosen in the person's memory. The rankings stay, permanently, as a small piece of them in the world.

It works for any occasion where people come together to honour someone. A memorial. A funeral. A birthday. A retirement. Anywhere people gather to say: this person matters.

---

## The reveal

Each poll carries a secret. You know something about the person being honoured — their favourite colour, the song they always hummed, the film they watched every Christmas — and you keep it back.

Guests see the question: *"Belinda had a colour she returned to all her life — what's yours?"*

They choose, they pledge. Then the answer appears.

*"Belinda's was purple. She wore it to every important occasion."*

The pledge becomes a two-way act. Your guest shares something of themselves. Belinda shares something back. It ends not with a receipt but with a moment.

You write the reveal when you create the favpoll. It's shown to every guest after they pledge — private to each of them, part of their experience of the day.

---

## What guests experience

Guests receive a link — by email, WhatsApp, or order of service. On their phone, they see the person's name, a question, and a list of options to pledge against.

They choose their favourites, split their donation however they like, and confirm. It takes two minutes. No app. No account required.

If you've written a reveal, it appears after they pay. If you haven't, they see the current rankings and their place in them.

---

## At the event itself

If you're holding a gathering — a service, a dinner, a party — you can display the live rankings on a screen. Guests watch in real time as pledges come in. Purple climbs because Belinda loved purple and the people who loved Belinda are saying so with their money.

It's unlike anything a collection tin can do.

---

## Planned in advance

Some people include a favpoll in their will. They choose the charity, write the questions, leave the reveals. When the time comes, an executor or family member follows the instructions and creates it.

If you're helping to carry out someone's wishes, favpoll is designed for exactly this. The questions and reveals can be as personal or as simple as the person intended. Everything else — the payments, the disbursement, the rankings — favpoll handles.

---

## The charity

You choose a charity that mattered to the person you're honouring — or simply one you believe in. Up to three charities can share the proceeds. When the poll closes, the money goes to them automatically. You receive a full summary of what was raised.

A 5% platform fee covers favpoll's costs. The remaining 95% reaches your chosen charity in full.

---

## The all-time record

Every pledge also contributes to favpoll's permanent all-time rankings — a growing record of what people love most, built entirely through charitable giving at life's most significant moments.

Your guests' answers join thousands of others. Their preferences become part of something larger. A small, lasting contribution from a day that meant everything.

---

## Create a favpoll

It takes about five minutes. You'll need:

- The name of the person you're honouring
- A charity (or up to three)
- A closing date for the poll

That's it. favpoll handles everything else.
